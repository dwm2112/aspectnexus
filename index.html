Our Mission

Bridging the STEM Opportunity Gap in Rural America

AspectNexus is a non-profit organization dedicated to providing accessible, quality STEM education to rural communities. Our mission is to empower individuals in rural areas to succeed in the 21st-century economy — regardless of their zip code.

We understand that rural communities face unique challenges: geographic isolation, limited institutional resources, and fewer pathways into technology and innovation. Our programs are designed to be flexible, community-rooted, and tailored to local needs.

We work in partnership with schools, educators, and community organizations to develop programs that actually address those needs — not a one-size-fits-all curriculum delivered from a distance.

Impact Areas

Three Pillars of Transformation

Foster in each student the full capacity, creativity, and confidence to become agents of change in their personal lives and in their community.

01

Inspiration

Inviting students to participate in the creative economy and to take ownership of their own future. We plant seeds of curiosity that grow into careers, ventures, and community leadership.

02

Innovation

Serving as a catalyst for grassroots invention. Our makerspaces and programs give rural students the tools to turn ideas into prototypes — and prototypes into reality.

Education

Our Goals

What We're Building

Our goals are grounded in research-backed education practice and shaped by the real needs of rural communities. They guide every program, partnership, and makerspace we create.

1

Create the context that develops the Maker mindset — a growth mindset that encourages every learner to believe they can learn to do anything.

2

Identify, develop, and share a broad framework of projects and kits based on a wide range of tools and materials that connect to student interests in and out of school.

3

Build a new body of practice in teaching making — and a corps of mentors and practitioners to follow it.

4

Design and develop Makerspaces across a variety of community contexts to serve diverse learners who don't share equal access to the same resources.

5

Design and host online platforms for collaboration among students, teachers, and the broader community.

6

Develop programs especially for young people that allow them to take a leading role in creating more Makers — and in shaping the community around them.

7

Create community contexts for exhibition and curation of student work, ensuring new opportunities are continuously created for more people to participate.

8

Allow individuals to build a record of participation in the Maker community — useful for academic and career advancement, and for personal growth.

9

Develop educational contexts that link the practice of making to formal concepts and theory, supporting exploration while introducing advanced design and new ways of thinking.

10

Foster in each student the full capacity, creativity, and confidence to become agents of change in their personal lives and in their community.

Makerspace

A Space Where Ideas Become Real

Our makerspaces are more than rooms full of tools. They're collaborative environments designed to spark curiosity, accelerate learning, and connect students with the people and ideas that shape the future.

Studio layouts that support individual initiative and autonomy, while encouraging cross-pollination of ideas across activity stations.

Past projects and current activities are visible and accessible — to seed ideas and provide inspiration for what's next.

Designed for diverse community contexts, serving learners who may not share equal access to the same institutional resources.

Community exhibitions that celebrate student work and invite more people into the making ecosystem.
